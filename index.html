Problems of electronic waste

  1. People are switching electronic devices much more frequently compared to the past due to the variety of new products released every year to lure people into buying new devices.
  2. Manufacturing companies make products with high fragility, which prompts customers to purchase warranties or to desire new products altogether.
  3. Old electronic devices are often left at home, and people usually forget about it. Very few electronic devices are properly recycled.
  4. The lack of awareness of E-waste recycling is alarming.

Definition of E-waste

Electronic landfill, coined “e-waste”, is a growing global issue that affects environment worldwide. E-waste is the collection of unwanted or “out-of-date” products that are replaced by newer or better models in technological advancements (i.e. changing iPhone 5 → iPhone 6 → iPhone X). Most of these products have an expected life expectancy from purchase date to the release of a better version of the same product.
